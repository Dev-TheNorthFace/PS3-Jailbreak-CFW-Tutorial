# 💾 Replacing your PS3 hard drive (CFW)

If you replace your hard drive with a larger one, here’s what you need to do 👇

---

## 🔌 1. Prepare the USB drive

- Use a USB drive formatted in **FAT32**  
- Add the CFW update file that matches your version (e.g. **4.92 Evilnat**)

Required structure:
PS3/  
└── UPDATE/  
  └── PS3UPDAT.PUP  

---

## ⚙️ 2. Install the new hard drive

- Replace the PS3 hard drive  
- Turn on the console  

👉 The PS3 will automatically ask for a **system reinstallation**

---

## 🔄 3. Reinstall CFW

1. Plug in the USB drive  
2. Follow the on-screen instructions  
3. Install the firmware (CFW)  

---

## 📦 4. Reinstall software

⚠️ Important:

- All homebrew apps (MultiMAN, webMAN, etc.) will be deleted  
- You will need to reinstall your **PKG files**  

---

## 🎮 5. Reinstall games

- Games stored on the old hard drive will be lost  
- You will need to:

  - Transfer your games again  
  - Reinstall your PKG files  
  - Restore your ROMs  

---

## 💡 Tip (recommended)

👉 Before changing the hard drive:

- Back up your important data:
  - Saves (Apollo)  
  - Games  
  - Files  

---

# ⚠️ Important

- ❗ Always use the **same or newer version** of the CFW  
- ❗ Never install an incompatible firmware  
- ❗ Make sure your PS3 is still CFW compatible  

---

# 🎯 Simple summary

👉 Changing the hard drive =

- Reinstall CFW  
- Reinstall PKG files  
- Reinstall games
